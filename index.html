📜 MODULE 1: PHILIPPINE CONSTITUTION (1987) ▼

ARTICLE III - BILL OF RIGHTS (Key Sections)

  • Section 1: No person shall be deprived of life, liberty, or property without due process of law, nor shall any person be denied the equal protection of the laws
  • Section 2: Right against unreasonable searches and seizures - requires warrant issued by judge upon probable cause
  • Section 4: Freedom of speech, expression, and press, and the right to peaceably assemble and petition the government
  • Section 5: Freedom of religion - separation of church and state, free exercise and non-establishment clauses
  • Section 12: Rights of persons under investigation (Miranda rights) - right to remain silent, right to counsel, right to be informed
  • Section 14: Right to due process in criminal cases - presumption of innocence, right to be heard, right to counsel
  • Section 19: Prohibition against cruel, degrading, or inhuman punishment

OTHER IMPORTANT PROVISIONS

  • Article VI, Section 16: Each house shall determine its rules of proceedings
  • Article VII, Section 4: President and Vice-President term: 6 years, starting June 30
  • Article VIII, Section 1: Judicial power vested in Supreme Court and lower courts
  • Article IX: Constitutional Commissions - CSC, COMELEC, COA - independent
  • Article XI: Ombudsman - investigates public officials
⚖️ MODULE 2: R.A. 6713 - CODE OF CONDUCT ▼

THE 8 NORMS OF CONDUCT

  • 1. Commitment to Public Interest: Public interest over personal interest
  • 2. Professionalism: Competence, integrity, and excellence
  • 3. Justness and Sincerity: Fairness and sincerity in actions
  • 4. Political Neutrality: No partisan political activities
  • 5. Responsiveness to the Public: Prompt, courteous, efficient service
  • 6. Nationalism and Patriotism: Prioritize national interests
  • 7. Commitment to Democracy: Uphold democratic principles
  • 8. Simple Living: Avoid extravagant lifestyle

SALN REQUIREMENTS

  • File within 30 days after assumption of office
  • File every year on or before April 30
  • File within 30 days after separation from service
  • Available for public scrutiny
