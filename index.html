📜 MODULE 1: PHILIPPINE CONSTITUTION (1987) ▼

ARTICLE I - NATIONAL TERRITORY

  • The national territory comprises the Philippine archipelago, with all the islands and waters embraced therein, and all other territories over which the Philippines has sovereignty or jurisdiction
  • Consists of terrestrial, fluvial, and aerial domains, including its territorial sea, the seabed, the subsoil, the insular shelves, and other submarine areas
  • The waters around, between, and connecting the islands of the archipelago, regardless of their breadth and dimensions, form part of the internal waters of the Philippines

ARTICLE II - DECLARATION OF PRINCIPLES AND STATE POLICIES

  • Section 1: The Philippines is a democratic and republican State. Sovereignty resides in the people and all government authority emanates from them
  • Section 2: The Philippines renounces war as an instrument of national policy, adopts the generally accepted principles of international law as part of the law of the land
  • Section 3: Civilian authority is, at all times, supreme over the military
  • Section 4: The prime duty of the Government is to serve and protect the people
  • Section 5: The maintenance of peace and order, the protection of life, liberty, and property, and the promotion of the general welfare are essential for the enjoyment by all the people of the blessings of democracy
  • Section 6: The separation of Church and State is inviolable
  • Section 7-8: Independent foreign policy, freedom from nuclear weapons
  • Section 9-10: Social justice, health, and ecology
  • Section 11: Value of human dignity and human rights
  • Section 12: Family as foundation of the nation
  • Section 13: Role of youth in nation-building
  • Section 14: Women in nation-building
  • Section 15-16: Health and ecology
  • Section 17-19: Education, labor, economy
  • Section 20-22: Private sector, autonomous regions, and indigenous peoples

ARTICLE III - BILL OF RIGHTS (22 Sections)

Key Sections:
Sec 1: Due process and equal protection
Sec 2: Right against unreasonable searches and seizures
Sec 3: Privacy of communication
Sec 4: Freedom of speech, expression, and press
Sec 5: Freedom of religion
Sec 6: Liberty of abode and travel
Sec 7: Right to information
Sec 8: Right to form associations
Sec 9: Right to just compensation for private property
Sec 10: Non-impairment of contracts
Sec 11: Free access to courts
Sec 12: Rights of persons under investigation (Miranda rights)
Sec 13: Right to bail
Sec 14: Right to due process in criminal cases
Sec 15: Right to speedy disposition
Sec 16: Right to speedy trial
Sec 17: Right against self-incrimination
Sec 18: Political beliefs and involuntary servitude
Sec 19: Prohibition against cruel punishment
Sec 20: No imprisonment for debt
Sec 21: Right against double jeopardy
Sec 22: Ex post facto laws and bills of attainder prohibited

OTHER IMPORTANT ARTICLES

  • Article VI: Legislative Department - Senate (24 senators, 6-year terms), House of Representatives (3-year terms, max 3 terms)
  • Article VII: Executive Department - President (6-year term, no reelection), Vice President (6-year term, max 2 terms)
  • Article VIII: Judicial Department - Supreme Court (15 justices)
  • Article IX: Constitutional Commissions - Civil Service, COMELEC, COA
  • Article X: Local Government
  • Article XI: Accountability of Public Officers - Ombudsman, Impeachment
  • Article XII: National Economy and Patrimony

  • Article XIV: Education, Science, Technology, Arts, Culture, Sports
  • Article XV: The Family
  • Article XVI: General Provisions
  • Article XVII: Amendments or Revisions
  • Article XVIII: Transitory Provisions
⚖️ MODULE 2: R.A. 6713 - CODE OF CONDUCT AND ETHICAL STANDARDS ▼

THE 8 NORMS OF CONDUCT

  • 1. Commitment to Public Interest: Always uphold public interest over personal interest
  • 2. Professionalism: Perform duties with competence, integrity, and excellence
  • 3. Justness and Sincerity: Act with fairness and sincerity
  • 4. Political Neutrality: Do not engage in partisan political activities
  • 5. Responsiveness to the Public: Provide prompt, courteous, and efficient service
  • 6. Nationalism and Patriotism: Prioritize national interests
  • 7. Commitment to Democracy: Uphold democratic principles and processes
  • 8. Simple Living: Avoid extravagant lifestyle, live modestly

SALN (STATEMENT OF ASSETS, LIABILITIES, AND NET WORTH)

  • Must be filed within 30 days after assumption of office
  • Filed every year on or before April 30
  • Must be filed within 30 days after separation from service
  • Contents: real property, personal property, liabilities, business interests
  • Available for public scrutiny

PROHIBITED ACTS

  • Receiving gifts or favors from persons with government transactions
  • Engaging in private practice without authority
  • Disclosure of confidential information
  • Nepotism in appointments
  • Discrimination in service delivery
Remember: RA 6713 applies to all public officials and employees, including those in government-owned and controlled corporations.
🕊️ MODULE 3: PEACE AND HUMAN RIGHTS ▼

UNIVERSAL DECLARATION OF HUMAN RIGHTS (UDHR)

  • Adopted December 10, 1948 by the UN General Assembly
  • 30 articles covering civil, political, economic, social, and cultural rights
  • Key rights: right to life, liberty, security; freedom from slavery, torture; right to recognition before the law; right to privacy; freedom of movement; right to nationality; right to marry; right to own property; freedom of thought, conscience, religion; freedom of opinion and expression; right to assembly; right to participate in government; right to social security; right to work; right to education; right to cultural participation

INTERNATIONAL COVENANTS

  • ICCPR: International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights
  • ICESCR: International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights
  • Both adopted in 1966, entered into force in 1976

PHILIPPINE COMMISSION ON HUMAN RIGHTS (CHR)

  • Created under Article XIII of the 1987 Constitution
  • Independent office
  • Powers: investigate human rights violations, provide legal aid, monitor compliance, recommend measures
  • Cannot prosecute cases - recommends to prosecutor

PEACE CONCEPTS

  • Negative Peace: Absence of war or direct violence
  • Positive Peace: Presence of justice, equality, and social harmony
  • Structural Violence: Systemic inequalities embedded in social structures
  • Cultural Violence: Aspects of culture that justify direct or structural violence
  • Peace Education: Teaching conflict resolution, tolerance, human rights, disarmament

📝 MODULE 5: VERBAL ABILITY (ENGLISH & FILIPINO) ▼

GRAMMAR RULES

  • Subject-Verb Agreement: Singular subjects take singular verbs; plural subjects take plural verbs
  • Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement: Pronouns must agree in number and gender with their antecedents
  • Tenses: Present, past, future, present perfect, past perfect, future perfect
  • Active vs Passive Voice: Active is direct and stronger; passive emphasizes the action
  • Modifiers: Place modifiers close to words they modify to avoid confusion
  • Parallelism: Use the same grammatical form for equal ideas

COMMONLY CONFUSED WORDS

  • Effect/Affect: Effect is noun (result); Affect is verb (to influence)
  • Their/There/They're: Their (possessive), There (place), They're (they are)
  • Your/You're: Your (possessive), You're (you are)
  • Its/It's: Its (possessive), It's (it is)
  • To/Too/Two: To (preposition), Too (also/excessively), Two (number)
  • Then/Than: Then (time), Than (comparison)
  • Loose/Lose: Loose (not tight), Lose (mislay, fail to win)

FILIPINO GRAMMAR

  • Pang-uri (Adjectives): Describe nouns - maganda, malaki, mabait
  • Pang-abay (Adverbs): Describe verbs, adjectives - mabilis, dahan-dahan
  • Pandiwa (Verbs): Action words - tumakbo, kumain, natulog
  • Panghalip (Pronouns): ako, ikaw, siya, tayo, kayo, sila
  • Pang-ukol (Prepositions): sa, para sa, ayon sa
Paragraph Organization: Chronological order, spatial order, order of importance, cause and effect, problem-solution.
🔢 MODULE 6: NUMERICAL ABILITY ▼

BASIC OPERATIONS

Addition (+) · Subtraction (-) · Multiplication (×) · Division (÷)
Order of Operations: PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction)

PERCENTAGES

  • Percentage = (Part/Whole) × 100%
  • Part = (Percentage × Whole)/100
  • Percentage Change = [(New - Original)/Original] × 100%
  • Discount = Original Price × (Discount Rate/100)
  • Sale Price = Original Price - Discount
  • Markup = Cost × (Markup Rate/100)
  • Selling Price = Cost + Markup

NUMBER SEQUENCES

  • Arithmetic Sequence: Constant difference (2,5,8,11... d=3)
  • Geometric Sequence: Constant ratio (3,6,12,24... r=2)
  • Fibonacci Sequence: Each term is sum of two preceding (1,1,2,3,5,8...)
  • Square Numbers: 1,4,9,16,25,36...
  • Cube Numbers: 1,8,27,64,125...
  • Prime Numbers: 2,3,5,7,11,13,17,19,23...

WORD PROBLEM FORMULAS

  • Work Problems: 1/T = 1/A + 1/B (together); T = (A×B)/(A+B)
  • Distance: Distance = Speed × Time; Speed = Distance/Time; Time = Distance/Speed
  • Average: Average = Sum of terms/Number of terms
  • Weighted Average: (w₁x₁ + w₂x₂)/(w₁ + w₂)
  • Simple Interest: I = P × R × T; A = P + I
  • Compound Interest: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
  • Ratio and Proportion: a:b = c:d → ad = bc
  • Age Problems: Let x = current age, use relationships
  • Mixture Problems: (Q₁×C₁ + Q₂×C₂) = Q_total × C_total
Example Work Problem: If A can do a job in 4 days and B in 6 days, together: T = (4×6)/(4+6) = 24/10 = 2.4 days
🧠 MODULE 7: ANALYTICAL ABILITY (PROFESSIONAL) ▼

WORD ANALOGY

  • Synonym: Happy : Joyful :: Sad : Gloomy
  • Antonym: Hot : Cold :: Light : Dark
  • Part-Whole: Chapter : Book :: Room : House
  • Cause-Effect: Study : Learn :: Practice : Improve
  • Function: Teacher : Teach :: Doctor : Heal
  • Degree: Warm : Hot :: Cool : Cold
  • Classification: Dog : Mammal :: Eagle : Bird
  • Tool-User: Hammer : Carpenter :: Scalpel : Surgeon

LOGICAL REASONING

  • Deductive Reasoning: General to specific (All men are mortal. Socrates is a man. Therefore, Socrates is mortal.)
  • Inductive Reasoning: Specific to general (The sun rose today, yesterday, always → sun always rises)
  • Syllogism: Major premise + Minor premise = Conclusion
  • Conditional Statements: If p then q (p→q). Contrapositive: If not q then not p
  • Fallacies: Circular reasoning, false cause, hasty generalization, ad hominem, straw man

ASSUMPTIONS AND CONCLUSIONS

  • Assumption: Unstated premise that must be true for argument to hold
  • Conclusion: Main point or inference drawn from premises
  • Strengthen/Weaken: Identify evidence that supports or undermines argument
  • Necessary vs Sufficient: Necessary condition must be present; sufficient condition guarantees outcome

DATA INTERPRETATION

  • Tables: Read rows and columns, find patterns, calculate percentages
  • Graphs: Bar graphs (comparisons), line graphs (trends), pie charts (proportions)
  • Mean, Median, Mode: Mean (average), Median (middle), Mode (most frequent)
  • Range: Highest - Lowest
  • Trend Analysis: Increase, decrease, fluctuate, stabilize
  • Projections: Extrapolate from given data
📂 MODULE 8: CLERICAL ABILITY (SUBPROFESSIONAL) ▼

FILING RULES

  • Alphabetical Order: Compare word by word, letter by letter
  • Names with Titles: File by last name, then first name, ignoring titles (Dr., Mr., Mrs.)
  • Mc and Mac: File as spelled (McDonald, MacDonald)
  • St. and Saint: File as "Saint" (St. Paul = Saint Paul)
  • Numbers: File as if spelled out (10th = Tenth)
  • Government Agencies: File under key word (Department of Education = Education)
  • Nothing Comes Before Something: Smith before Smithson
  • Hyphenated Names: Consider as one unit

SPELLING RULES

  • I before E except after C, or when sounding like A as in neighbor and weigh
  • Double consonants: accommodate, committee, necessary, occurrence
  • Silent letters: psychology, pneumonia, receipt, doubt
  • -able vs -ible: acceptable, available (able); possible, visible (ible)
  • -ance vs -ence: attendance, importance (ance); existence, dependence (ence)

COMMONLY MISSPELLED WORDS

  • accommodate (not accomodate)
  • embarrass (not embarass)
  • millennium (not millenium)
  • publicly (not publically)
  • receive (not recieve)
  • separate (not seperate)
  • privilege (not privelege)
  • occurrence (not occurence)
  • necessary (not neccessary)
  • weird (not wierd)
